CleverBirds
A Multiple-Choice Benchmark for Fine-grained Human Knowledge Tracing
Leonie Bossemeyer1 Samuel Heinrich2 Grant Van Horn3 Oisin Mac Aodha1
1University of Edinburgh 2Cornell University 3UMass Amherst

CleverBirds


Mastering fine-grained visual recognition, essential in many expert domains, can require that specialists undergo years of dedicated training. Modeling the progression of such expertize in humans remains challenging, and accurately inferring a human learnerâ€™s knowledge state is a key step toward understanding visual learning. We introduce CleverBirds, a large-scale knowledge tracing benchmark for fine-grained bird species recognition. Collected by the citizen-science platform eBird, it offers insight into how individuals acquire expertize in complex fine-grained classification. More than 40,000 participants have engaged in the quiz, answering over 17 million multiple-choice questions spanning over 10,000 bird species, with long-range learning patterns across an average of 400 questions per participant. We release this dataset to support the development and evaluation of new methods for visual knowledge tracing. We show that tracking learners' knowledge is challenging, especially across participant subgroups and question types, with different forms of contextual information offering varying degrees of predictive benefit. CleverBirds is among the largest benchmark of its kind, offering a substantially higher number of learnable concepts. With it, we hope to enable new avenues for studying the development of visual expertize over time and across individuals.

Dataset
Quiz Composition
The CleverBirds dataset is sourced from an online bird species identification quiz created by the citizen science project eBird. Users are shown an image and asked to guess which species of bird from a list of options is present in the image shown.
Quiz images are sampled from images uploaded within the last 5 to 365 days and contain proportions of both images with no quality rank, and images that have quality rank of at least 2.4 out of 5 possible stars.
Data Filtering
CleverBirds is based on all quizzes completed online from March 14th 2018 to October 8th, 2024. To maintain a focus on visual learning, we exclude audio-based quizzes, questions missing valid image assets, and unanswered questions.
To limit the influence of less committed users, we remove data from users with less than two attempted quizzes and retain only the first 350 quizzes per user.
Privacy & Release
To ensure user privacy we anonymize all user-related identifiers, as well as those of quizzes, questions, and image assets. We aggregate quiz locations using the H3 geospatial index at resolution 3.
We release interaction histories with anonymized participant IDs for all questions in the filtered dataset, alongside precomputed image features for all the image assets.
